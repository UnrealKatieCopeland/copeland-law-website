# Blog Post Series Strategy: Appellate Research Content

## Overview
Katie's appellate research contains rich material for a comprehensive blog post series that can:
- Educate judges, attorneys, and advocates
- Build SEO authority for the website
- Position Katie as a thought leader in ADA courtroom compliance
- Create shareable content for social media
- Support future CLE course development

## Proposed Blog Post Series (12+ Posts)

### **Series 1: Legal Foundations (4 posts)**

#### 1. "Void vs. Voidable Orders: What's the Difference and Why It Matters"
**Target audience:** Attorneys, judges, law students  
**Key concepts:**
- Definition of void orders (lack of subject matter jurisdiction)
- Definition of voidable orders (procedural defects)
- Bridgman v. Moore: Texas courts' duty to vacate void orders
- Why the distinction matters for ADA accommodation denials
- Collateral attack implications

**SEO keywords:** void orders, voidable orders, subject matter jurisdiction, Bridgman v. Moore, Texas appellate law

---

#### 2. "The State-Created Danger Doctrine: When Courts Create Harm"
**Target audience:** Civil rights attorneys, disability advocates  
**Key concepts:**
- Origins in DeShaney v. Winnebago County
- Four elements of state-created danger
- "Functional custody" concept
- Application to ADA accommodation denials
- Direct causation from denial to documented harm

**SEO keywords:** state-created danger doctrine, DeShaney, 42 USC 1983, civil rights liability, functional custody

---

#### 3. "Structural Error vs. Harmless Error in ADA Cases"
**Target audience:** Appellate attorneys, judges  
**Key concepts:**
- Arizona v. Fulminante framework
- Why ADA denials are structural, not trial errors
- Automatic reversal vs. harmless error analysis
- Corruption of the entire proceeding
- No reliable result possible without accommodations

**SEO keywords:** structural error, harmless error, Arizona v. Fulminante, appellate review, ADA violations

---

#### 4. "Federal Rule 60(b)(4) and Void Orders: A Pathway to Relief"
**Target audience:** Attorneys seeking post-judgment relief  
**Key concepts:**
- Fed. R. Civ. P. 60(b)(4) overview
- No time limit for void order challenges
- Jurisdictional defects cannot be waived
- Strategic use in ADA cases
- Distinguishing from other Rule 60(b) grounds

**SEO keywords:** Rule 60(b)(4), post-judgment relief, void orders, federal civil procedure, jurisdictional defects
